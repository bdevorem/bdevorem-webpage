About Me


I'm from Northern California and I love winter. Just kidding.

In all seriousness, I do enjoy living in Indiana because that's where I get to explore my passions. I am absolutely in love with computer science. I'm lucky enough to be in a position where I get to learn something new each day (multiple times a day), and at every moment I become even more passionate. The more I learn, the more I want to learn.

I've always been interested in cyber security, but I've recently figured out that I'm more interested in the practice of creating secure code and software, and the theory behind why such a computation exists and is necessary. My interests are not just singular though: programming language theory, theoretical computer science, automata theory, software development, and hacking on all things Linux, just to name a few.

I was introduced to computer science research last summer, and since then I have not been able to invision a future consisting of anything besides research. After graduation, I plan to head to graduate school to do just that: pursue some neat and engaging research (and a degree) in a mix between programming language theory and security. Well, that's the plan for now.

Last summer, I lived in Ireland where I interned at IBM Research Dublin. That was my first experience both traveling outside of the US and working on a research project, and it was phenomenal. Working on computer science research opened my eyes to parts of computer science that I had never seen before and showed me that there is so much that I don't know that I would actually love. Not only do I feel like a better programmer after my internship, but I also feel like a more well-rounded person. It's so easy to get caught up in assignments and projects during the academic year and forget the overall goal and lose perspective. However, last summer I learned how to take a step back from the everyday coding projects and take it all in. After a summer of not being a student, I somehow learned how to be a better student.

Just next month, I will be interning for IBM at the Almaden Valley Research Center in San Jose. I'm truly excited to step foot in that company once again, because I know I'm going to learn so much, just like last year. Wish me luck!
