About Me


I'm from Northern California and I love winter. Just kidding.

In all seriousness, I do enjoy living in Indiana because that's where I get to explore my passions. I am absolutely in love with computer science.

Moment of silence for that clich√©.

The feeling is actually quite hard to explain... It's like I just can't get enough of computer science. I keep yearning for more. Everything I have learned has intrigued me, tested me, pulled out the childlike curiosity in me - and I know I've only had a tiny taste of all that's out there. By the way, that tiny taste is better than any guac I have ever consumed. I'm lucky enough to be in a position where I get to satisfy my need to learn every single day, which just fuels my passions. The more I learn, the more I want to learn.


Cyber security is the field that constantly piques my interests, but I have always been a theory person. I am intrigued by the practice of creating secure code and software, however my main interests lie in programming languages and their construction, especially the area where security meets language design.
My interests are not just singular though. I love PL, but I also am interested in optimization, automata theory, systems, and hacking on all things Linux. I am also curious about software development, AI, and computer science education, but I lack experience in those fields.

In the summer of 2015, I lived in Ireland where I interned at IBM Research Dublin. That summer was phenomenal for many reasons, but mainly because it was my first experience performing computer science research. If there is anything in life that I am meant to do, it's to spend my life studying computer science and trying to tackle those unanswered questions that may (will) keep me up for days on end. The work I did and the people I met allowed me to come to that realization, which solidified my previously shaky intent to pursue PL research and graduate education. Now, I can't imagine a future for myself that doesn't consist of research.
After that internship, I feel like I came alive in my education. My work made me a better coder and a more well-rounded person, but doing research gave me a strange energy that made me want to soak up every second of my education and every bit of knowledge that I absolutely could. If that doesn't tell you I'm meant for research, nothing else will.

For the summer of 2016, I am a research intern for IBM once again, this time at the Almaden Valley Research Center in San Jose. Stay tuned for updates!
