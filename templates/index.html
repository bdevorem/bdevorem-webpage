About Me


I'm from Northern California and I love winter. Just kidding.

In all seriousness, I do enjoy living in Indiana because that's where I get to explore my passions. I am absolutely in love with computer science. It seems easy and typical to state that sentence as a CS undergrad, but I feel like I can never get enough of computer science. I always want to learn more because I have only had a small taste of the vast expansion of knowledge right at my fingertips, and that small taste is better than any guac I have ever consumed. I'm lucky enough to be in a position where I get to satisfy my need to learn, every single day, which just fuels my passions. The more I learn, the more I want to learn.

Cyber security is the field that constantly piques my interests, but I have always been a theory person. I am intrigued by the practice of creating secure code and software, however my main interests lie in programming languages and their construction, especially the area where security meets language design.
My interests are not just singular though. I love PL, but I also am interested in optimization, automata theory, and hacking on all things Linux. I am also curious about software development, AI, and computer science education, but I lack experience in those fields.

In the summer of 2015, I lived in Ireland where I interned at IBM Research Dublin. That summer was phenomenal for many reasons, but mainly because it was my first experience performing computer science research. I had always thought about eventually pursuing a PhD, but I never knew exactly what it would entail or even if it was right for me. Well, the work I did and the people I met really solidified my intent to pursue PL research and graduate education, and now I feel like I was made for it. I can't imagine a future for myself that doesn't consist of research.
After that internship, I felt like a better coded and a more well-rounded person. I learned how easy it is to get caught up in assignments and projects during the academic year, but more importantly I learned how to take a step back and gain some perspective. After a summer of not being a student, I somehow learned how to be a better student.

For the summer of 2016, I am a research intern for IBM once again, this time at the Almaden Valley Research Center in San Jose. Stay tuned for updates!
